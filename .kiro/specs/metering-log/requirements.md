# Requirements — Metering Log (Layer 2)

## Introduction

The **Metering Log** is a per-shard, append-only, signed hash chain that
captures fine-grained metered events (resource consumption, micro-debits,
micro-credits) at line rate **without** writing every event to the asset
ledger. It periodically flushes a compact, verifiable summary
(`BatchSettlementAction`) to the Layer-3 programmable asset ledger.

The metering log exists to solve three problems at once:

1. **Throughput.** The asset ledger is global, replicated, and consensus-
   gated. Writing every microjoule charge to it is a non-starter.
2. **Auditability.** Operators (and counterparties) must be able to verify
   that a settlement summary faithfully represents the underlying events,
   including under adversarial replay or selective omission.
3. **Tamper-evidence under partial trust.** A shard's process may be
   compromised in the future; the chain must make that detectable rather
   than rewritable.

The metering log is **generic** — it carries opaque event payloads — but its
first consumer is **Joule resource credits**, and the design is validated
against that workload.

This spec depends on the precursor refactor
(`asset-account-store-generalization`) and the Layer-3 asset ledger
(`programmable-asset-ledger`).

## Glossary

- **Shard**: A single process/replica that owns a contiguous subset of
  members and writes one metering log file. Sharding key is `memberId`.
- **Process key**: An ephemeral Ed25519 keypair generated by a shard at
  startup. Used to sign metering-log records. Certified in the asset ledger
  via `ProcessKeyCertAction` and revoked via `ProcessKeyRevokeAction`.
- **Record**: One length-prefixed CBOR-encoded entry in the metering log.
- **Tip**: The hash of the latest record in a shard's chain (cumulative).
- **Signing window (K)**: Every Kth record is signed by the process key.
  K is configurable (default 64; max 256).
- **Batch window**: The time or record-count interval after which the
  shard emits a `BatchSettlementAction` to the asset ledger.
- **BatchSettlementAction**: Layer-3 ledger action carrying
  `{ shardId, fromSeq, toSeq, tipHash, itemsRoot, memberDeltas[] }`.
- **memberDelta**: Per-member net `(earned, spent)` over the batch window
  for one assetId.
- **itemsRoot**: Merkle root of all records in the batch window, allowing
  selective disclosure of individual events to a counterparty.
- **Capability_Flag**: Boolean configuration flag enabling a feature;
  defaults to OFF unless preserving prior behavior.

## Requirement 1 — Per-Shard Append-Only Log

**User Story:** As a system operator, I need each shard to own a single,
append-only chain so that ordering is local, throughput is high, and
crash-recovery is straightforward.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- 1.1 The system SHALL create exactly one metering log file per shard,
  identified by `shardId: string` (UUIDv7 generated at first start).
- 1.2 Records SHALL be appended in monotonically increasing `seq` (uint64,
  starts at 0 for the genesis record).
- 1.3 The system SHALL prevent concurrent writers via OS file lock; second
  writer SHALL fail to open with `MeteringLogLockedError`.
- 1.4 Reads SHALL be safe under concurrent writes (writer fsyncs before
  advertising tip; reader reads only up to advertised tip).
- 1.5 On crash, the recovery procedure SHALL truncate any trailing partial
  record (one whose length prefix says N bytes but fewer than N follow)
  and SHALL log a `MeteringLogRecovery` event.
- 1.6 The log SHALL be storage-engine pluggable: a flat-file implementation
  is mandatory for v1; an LMDB implementation is permitted but not required.

## Requirement 2 — Record Format

**User Story:** As an auditor, I need every record to carry enough metadata
to reconstruct the chain and verify signatures without external state.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- 2.1 Each record SHALL be a length-prefixed CBOR map with fields:
  `seq: uint64`, `prev_hash: bytes32`, `ts: uint64` (microseconds since
  epoch), `op: string` (event discriminator), `memberId: bytes32`,
  `assetId: string`, `amount: bigint` (microunits, signed), `opId: string`
  (caller-provided idempotency key), `context_hash: bytes32` (opaque,
  caller-supplied).
- 2.2 The genesis record (`seq=0`) SHALL have `prev_hash` equal to
  32 zero bytes and SHALL carry the `shardId` and the activating process
  key fingerprint as its `context_hash`.
- 2.3 `prev_hash` of every non-genesis record SHALL equal
  `BLAKE3(serialized_previous_record)`. (BLAKE3 chosen for speed.)
- 2.4 The length prefix SHALL be a uint32 little-endian byte count of the
  CBOR payload (excluding the prefix itself), enabling fast scan-forward.
- 2.5 The system SHALL reject duplicate `(memberId, opId)` within a single
  batch window; recovery from idempotent retry SHALL return the original
  `seq`.
- 2.6 Records SHALL NOT include the signature inline. Signatures are
  out-of-band (see Requirement 3) so verification scope is explicit.

## Requirement 3 — Signing Cadence (Every Kth Record)

**User Story:** As an auditor, I need cryptographic anchors throughout the
chain so that selective forgery requires forging a signature, not just a
hash.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- 3.1 The system SHALL emit a `Signature` entry every K records where
  K is configurable (default 64, min 16, max 256).
- 3.2 A `Signature` entry SHALL bind: `seq` of latest record, `tipHash`
  (cumulative chain hash), `processKeyFingerprint`, and an Ed25519
  signature over `BLAKE3("metering-log-sig-v1" || shardId || seq ||
  tipHash || processKeyFingerprint)`.
- 3.3 `Signature` entries SHALL be stored alongside the log in a sidecar
  file `<shardId>.sigs` indexed by `seq`. Tampering with the sidecar
  SHALL be detectable via the asset-ledger-anchored fingerprints.
- 3.4 Verifying a record range SHALL require: (a) sequential hash chain
  validity, and (b) at least one valid `Signature` entry covering a `seq`
  ≥ the range's max.
- 3.5 The system SHALL emit a final `Signature` entry on graceful
  shutdown regardless of K-cadence. On crash, the most recent
  `Signature` covers up to the last K-aligned record only; the unsigned
  tail is reported but not denied.

## Requirement 4 — Process Key Lifecycle (via Asset Ledger)

**User Story:** As a security engineer, I need ephemeral process keys to
be globally certified and revocable so that a compromised shard's signing
power has bounded blast radius.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- 4.1 On startup, a shard SHALL generate a fresh Ed25519 keypair and
  SHALL submit a `ProcessKeyCertAction { shardId, fingerprint, pubKey,
  notBefore, notAfter }` to the asset ledger, signed by the operator's
  long-term signer.
- 4.2 The shard SHALL refuse to write metering records until its
  `ProcessKeyCertAction` has been confirmed in the asset ledger by at
  least one finality block.
- 4.3 On graceful shutdown the shard SHALL submit
  `ProcessKeyRevokeAction { shardId, fingerprint, reason: 'rotation' }`.
- 4.4 An operator SHALL be able to submit
  `ProcessKeyRevokeAction { ..., reason: 'compromise', effectiveAtSeq:
  uint64 }` to retroactively repudiate records after `effectiveAtSeq`.
- 4.5 Verifiers SHALL ignore `Signature` entries whose `processKeyFingerprint`
  is revoked with effect at or before the signed `seq`.
- 4.6 `notAfter` SHALL be at most 7 days. Long-running shards SHALL rotate
  by chaining a `Signature` over the rotation point and then emitting a
  fresh `ProcessKeyCertAction`.

## Requirement 5 — Batch Settlement to Asset Ledger

**User Story:** As an asset ledger operator, I need a compact verifiable
summary of a metering shard's activity so that I can post settlement
without replaying the full event stream.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- 5.1 The system SHALL emit a `BatchSettlementAction` per shard per batch
  window. Window SHALL trigger on whichever comes first: (a) `maxRecords`
  (default 10_000), (b) `maxAgeMs` (default 5_000 ms), (c) explicit
  `flush()` call.
- 5.2 The action SHALL carry `{ shardId, fromSeq, toSeq, tipHash,
  itemsRoot, memberDeltas: Array<{ memberId, assetId, earned: bigint,
  spent: bigint }> }`.
- 5.3 `itemsRoot` SHALL be the Merkle root of the records' BLAKE3 leaf
  hashes in `seq` order. The Merkle tree SHALL be a binary, balanced,
  RFC-9162 (Certificate Transparency) compatible variant for inclusion-
  proof reuse.
- 5.4 `memberDeltas` SHALL be sorted by `(memberId, assetId)` and
  deduplicated (one row per `(memberId, assetId)` per batch).
- 5.5 The asset ledger SHALL reject a `BatchSettlementAction` whose
  `fromSeq != previousBatchToSeq + 1` (or `0` for the first batch from
  a shard).
- 5.6 The asset ledger SHALL reject a `BatchSettlementAction` whose
  `tipHash` does not match a recomputation of the chain tip from
  `fromSeq` to `toSeq` over the records the operator presents on
  challenge. The default mode is **trust-but-verifiable**: settlement is
  accepted optimistically, and any party may request a challenge within
  the dispute window.
- 5.7 A `BatchSettlementAction` SHALL include the most recent valid
  `Signature` entry covering `toSeq` so that verifiers can authenticate
  the settlement without holding the full sidecar.

## Requirement 6 — Selective Disclosure & Inclusion Proofs

**User Story:** As a counterparty disputing a charge, I need to prove that
a specific record either is or is not in a settled batch without needing
the operator to disclose every other member's events.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- 6.1 The system SHALL provide `proveInclusion(shardId, seq): InclusionProof`
  returning the Merkle path from the record leaf to the batch's
  `itemsRoot`.
- 6.2 The system SHALL provide `proveExclusion(shardId, memberId, opId,
  fromSeq, toSeq): ExclusionProof` returning a sorted-leaf scan
  certificate.
- 6.3 Inclusion proofs SHALL be verifiable using only the public
  `BatchSettlementAction` and the proof itself (no log access required).
- 6.4 The on-disk Merkle index SHALL persist enough internal nodes that
  proof generation is O(log N) per query without re-reading every record.

## Requirement 7 — Dispute Window

**User Story:** As an operator, I need a defined window during which
settlements can be challenged so that downstream balances become final
in bounded time.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- 7.1 The system SHALL expose a per-asset `disputeWindowMs` (default
  86_400_000 = 24h) configurable on the asset ledger asset record.
- 7.2 During the window any party SHALL be able to submit
  `BatchChallengeAction { settlementBatchId, claimedDiscrepancy }`.
- 7.3 The shard operator SHALL respond with the disputed record range and
  Merkle proofs within `disputeResponseMs` (default 6h).
- 7.4 Challenges SHALL be auto-resolved against the on-chain `tipHash`
  and `itemsRoot`; an operator that fails to respond SHALL have the
  settlement marked `DISPUTED_NO_RESPONSE` and downstream balances
  rolled back.
- 7.5 After the window closes with no successful challenge, the
  settlement SHALL be marked `FINAL` and balances SHALL be considered
  permanent.

## Requirement 8 — Operational-Tier Coupling (AssetAccountStore)

**User Story:** As a runtime engineer, I need the operational store to
reflect metering-log activity in real time, with the documented lag
boundary that ledger settlement defines finality.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- 8.1 On every successful `appendRecord`, the metering log SHALL invoke
  `AssetAccountStore.applyDelta(memberId, assetId, earned, spent)`
  optimistically. Reservations are unchanged.
- 8.2 On `BatchSettlementAction` confirmation, the store SHALL update
  `getLastSettledAt(assetId)` to the batch's wall-clock time.
- 8.3 On `BatchChallengeAction` resolution against the operator, the
  store SHALL roll back deltas via the recorded reverse delta, keyed
  by `(shardId, fromSeq, toSeq)`.
- 8.4 The store SHALL never block on ledger settlement; metering events
  apply at append time. This is the documented eventual-consistency
  surface.

## Requirement 9 — Performance Targets

**User Story:** As a capacity planner, I need numbers I can plan against.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- 9.1 A single shard SHALL sustain ≥ 50_000 records/sec on commodity
  NVMe with `maxRecords=10_000`, `maxAgeMs=5_000`.
- 9.2 Append latency at the 99th percentile SHALL be < 5 ms under
  steady-state load.
- 9.3 `BatchSettlementAction` size SHALL scale with **distinct
  `(memberId, assetId)` pairs**, not record count, and SHALL be
  ≤ 256 KiB at p99 with 10_000 records and ≤ 1_000 distinct pairs.
- 9.4 Inclusion proof generation SHALL be < 10 ms p99.

## Requirement 10 — Test Coverage

- 10.1 Property-based: random sequences of `appendRecord` followed by
  `flush` SHALL produce a `BatchSettlementAction` whose Merkle root,
  recomputed independently from the record stream, matches.
- 10.2 Property-based: arbitrary subsets of records SHALL pass inclusion
  proof verification; non-included records SHALL pass exclusion proof
  verification.
- 10.3 Crash recovery: a record truncated at every byte offset SHALL
  result in a recoverable log whose tip is the last fully-written
  record.
- 10.4 Adversarial: a malicious sidecar with a forged `Signature`
  entry over a divergent chain SHALL be rejected by the verifier.
- 10.5 Adversarial: a settlement claiming a `tipHash` inconsistent with
  the underlying records SHALL be rejected by the asset ledger
  challenge path.
- 10.6 Full BrightChain test suite SHALL pass green at PR merge time.

## Requirement 11 — Brand Vocabulary (Inherited)

- 11.1 No forbidden terms (`coin`, `holder`, `tokenomics`, `airdrop`,
  `staking`, `marketCap`).
- 11.2 Verbs allowed: `earn`, `spend`, `transfer`, `reserve`, `settle`,
  `release`, `credit`, `debit`, `attest`, `flush`, `seal`.

## Out of Scope (Explicit)

- Cross-shard ordering or merging. Shards are independent.
- Replication of the metering log itself (shards are SPOFs by design;
  the asset ledger is the durable record after settlement).
- Witness cosigning à la Sigsum (deferred to a future "metering-log
  witnesses" spec; the current design is optimistic-with-challenge).
- Encryption of record payloads (caller responsibility via
  `context_hash`; the log is integrity-protected only).
- Compaction / pruning of historical log files (deferred; settled
  batches may be archived externally).
- Joule-specific rate tables (live in `joule-resource-credits` spec).
