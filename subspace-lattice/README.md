# Subspace Lattice

A modern hybrid board game, combining the spatial territory control of Go with the individual piece agency of Chess. This game serves as a demo of BrightStack and the messaging/gossip protocols baked into BrightChain.

## The Core Concept
In Subspace Lattice, the board is a living network. Strategy isn't just about where you are; it's about how you are projecting energy. The game moves the Go/Chess hybrid into a "space-grid" where the board itself is a living network.

### 1. The Board: The Sector Grid
Instead of a flat grid, imagine a 9x9 or 11x11 board where the intersections are called "Coordinates." Some coordinates are "Gravity Wells" (Stars or Planets) that cannot be occupied but affect the lines of movement around them.

### 2. The Pieces: The "Fleet"
The pieces are sleek, minimalist starship markers. Each player has a Command Hub (The Enterprise/Flagship).

| Piece | The "Chess" Role | The "Go" Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Command Hub** | The King | Radiates a "Sensor Net" 2 spaces in every direction. |
| **Escorts** | The Pawns/Sentries | Can move 1 space, but their main job is to Relay. |
| **Infiltrators** | The Knights | Can "warp" to any empty coordinate that is not currently inside an enemy Sensor Net. |
| **Beams** | The Rooks | Can move any distance in a straight line, but only within your own Sensor Net. |

### 3. The "Classic" Hybrid Mechanic: The Sensor Net
This is where Go meets the 24th Century.

* **Establishing the Net:** In Go, you surround territory with stones. In Subspace Lattice, you surround territory with Signals. If your pieces are within 2 spaces of each other, they "link."
* **The Logic:** Any empty Coordinate "enclosed" by your linked ships becomes your Sovereign Space.
* **Tactical Advantage:** Your ships move faster and can "warp" within Sovereign Space. If an enemy ship enters your Sovereign Space, it is "Detected"—it loses its special abilities and can only move 1 square at a time as it tries to escape.

### 4. Victory: "The Prime Directive" vs. "Total Control"
To make it feel like a modern classic, there are two ways to win:

1. **Surgical Strike:** Capture/Disable the enemy Command Hub.
2. **Sector Integration:** Control 51% of the coordinates on the board by the time all pieces have been deployed.

## Why it feels like a "Star Trek" Strategy Game:

* **Resource Management:** You have to decide: do I use my Escorts to expand my "Sensor Net" (Territory), or do I cluster them around my Command Hub for defense?
* **The "Cloak" Maneuver:** You can move an Infiltrator out of your sensor net to "cut" the opponent's link. If you successfully break their line, their entire forward fleet loses its "Net" and becomes vulnerable.
* **Visual Appeal:** A board made of dark glass or acrylic with etched lines. The pieces could be brushed metal.
